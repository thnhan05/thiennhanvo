About Me

I am an undergraduate research student in Robotics and Artificial Intelligence at HUTECH University, currently affiliated with the Information Security Laboratory (InSec Lab) at the University of Information Technology, Vietnam National University, Ho Chi Minh City. My research interests span large language models (LLMs), privacy-preserving AI, smart contract security, and brain–computer interfaces (BCI).

I am currently working on LLM-augmented fuzzing for smart contract vulnerability detection, integrating agentic AI workflows to automate test generation, exploit reasoning, and multi-step analysis of Solidity codebases. In parallel, I investigate privacy attacks and leakage prevention in AI systems, with a focus on LLM reminder-based techniques.

My interdisciplinary portfolio also includes EEG/fNIRS signal processing for BCI systems, AI-based Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) for photovoltaic energy, and hyperspectral imaging for precision agriculture — reflecting a commitment to research that bridges theory and real-world impact.

TRDP-EEG: Time-Resolved Decoding of Perceived and Imagined Visual Object States from EEG
Under Review
Vo, T. N., Tran, X.-T.
NeurIPS 2026 Workshop · Under Review
5 Temporal Windows · 100–700 ms Peak Accuracy 98.49% EEG-to-Image CNN · Perception vs. Imagination
A time-resolved decoding framework for distinguishing Perception vs. Imagination of visual objects from EEG. Converts EEG channels into 2D time–channel images for lightweight CNN classification across five overlapping windows. Achieves up to 98.49% mean accuracy (200–700 ms), substantially outperforming ML/DL baselines — revealing that discriminative neural signatures emerge strongly after 200 ms post-stimulus.

TinyCNNDeep: Lightweight Attention-Based CNN for EEG Classification of Eye States and Sleep Deprivation
Thien Nhan Vo, Yen Nhi Tran Thi, Ngan Nguyen Xuan Phuong, Xuan-The Tran
arXiv · arXiv:2606.26506 [eess.SP] · 2026
A lightweight CNN combining residual learning with Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) attention for four-class EEG classification (normal/deprived × eyes-open/closed). Achieves 83.69% subject-wise mean accuracy on a 35-subject dataset, outperforming the strongest baseline (Random Forest, 47.66%) by +36.03 pp, using only 5 frontal–occipital EEG channels and an image-based 2D convolution representation.
