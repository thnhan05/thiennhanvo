### CombinedCNN: Lightweight Spatial-Temporal Attention Network for EEG Intent Recognition

**Status:** 🎉 Accepted at IEEE ICCE 2026

**Authors:** **Thien Nhan Vo** et al.

**Venue:** *IEEE International Conference on Consumer Electronics (ICCE) 2026*  
Selected for the Special Session on *Intelligent Multimodal Perception and Cognitive Engineering*

**Highlights:**
- Joint Spatial–Temporal CNN + Multi-Head Attention
- Balanced Accuracy **0.820** · ITR 1.299 bits/min
- ~10× Fewer Parameters vs. Standard 2D CNN

A lightweight BCI network that jointly models **spatial electrode relationships** (2D conv) and **temporal event-related dynamics** (1D conv) in a unified end-to-end pipeline with attention-based refinement. Evaluated on the RSVP-BCI benchmark, it challenges the complexity–performance trade-off: **higher accuracy at a fraction of the compute**.

### TRDP-EEG: Time-Resolved Decoding of Perceived and Imagined Visual Object States from EEG

**Status:** ⏳ Under Review

**Authors:** **Vo, T. N.**, Tran, X.-T.

**Venue:** *NeurIPS 2026 Workshop* · Under Review

**Highlights:**
- 5 Temporal Windows · 100–700 ms
- Peak Accuracy **98.49%**
- EEG-to-Image CNN · Perception vs. Imagination

A time-resolved decoding framework for distinguishing **Perception vs. Imagination** of visual objects from EEG. Converts EEG channels into 2D time–channel images for lightweight CNN classification across five overlapping windows. Achieves up to **98.49% mean accuracy** (200–700 ms), substantially outperforming ML/DL baselines — revealing that discriminative neural signatures emerge strongly **after 200 ms post-stimulus**.

### TinyCNNDeep: Lightweight Attention-Based CNN for EEG Classification of Eye States and Sleep Deprivation

**Authors:** **Thien Nhan Vo**, Yen Nhi Tran Thi, Ngan Nguyen Xuan Phuong, Xuan-The Tran

**Venue:** *arXiv* · arXiv:2606.26506 [eess.SP] · 2026

**Links:** [[arXiv]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26506) [[PDF]](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.26506)

A lightweight CNN combining residual learning with Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) attention for four-class EEG classification (normal/deprived × eyes-open/closed). Achieves **83.69%** subject-wise mean accuracy on a 35-subject dataset, outperforming the strongest baseline (Random Forest, 47.66%) by **+36.03 pp**, using only 5 frontal–occipital EEG channels and an image-based 2D convolution representation.
